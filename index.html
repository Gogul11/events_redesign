About The Club

The ACM-CEG Student Chapter, initiated in 2004, aims to instill an unwavering enthusiasm for computer science in students. The club provides a plethora of networking opportunities and helps to seek advice from the top experts in the field. The club has been steadily working to inculcate an unalloyed interest in Computer Science in students and consequently, stimulating the advancement of computer science as a whole.

Our Flagship Events

Prodigy, an annual state-level technical event organized by the ACM-CEG chapter at Anna University, is a pioneering initiative designed exclusively for students in grades 9-12. Rooted in the ethos of promoting technological awareness and fostering a passion for computer science, Prodigy goes beyond traditional education, providing a dynamic platform for young minds to delve into the exciting realms of computing and technology. Over the span of more than 15 years, Prodigy has transcended its identity as a mere technical event; it has evolved into a transformative experience. By reaching out to schools and students across the state, Prodigy has become a beacon of inspiration, sparking a passion for technology that extends far beyond the duration of the event. The legacy of Prodigy is etched in the minds of those who have participated, fostering a community of young enthusiasts who are not just consumers but creators in the world of technology.
Empowerment takes center stage at "CodHer," our exclusive women-only hackathon designed to inspire and motivate female developers to actively engage in the dynamic world of hackathons. Providing a dedicated platform for female talent, CodHer is a showcase of skills, innovation, and collaboration. With renowned sponsors like Motorq and GitHub, participants have the opportunity to compete for cash prizes exceeding 50k, along with the chance to secure coveted internships. Join CodHer and become a part of a supportive community, celebrating the prowess of women in the tech space and encouraging their active participation in the ever-evolving landscape of hackathons.

F.A.Q

  • What is ACM?

    The ACM-CEG Student Chapter is a recognized student organization dedicated to fostering a genuine passion for Computer Science among students. The club is actively working to spark interest in computer science, regardless of individuals' backgrounds and experiences and contribute to the overall advancement of the field

  • Are only CS students a part of this club?

    No, anyone from any department can be a part of this club.

  • What do we do?

    We meticulously curate an array of events, such as CODHER, dedicated to championing enhanced female representation in technology, PRODIGY, aimed at nurturing a fervor for computer science and intellectual thinking among school students. Additionally, we facilitate enlightening webinars and alumni talks to offer nuanced insights and learnings of new technologies and comprehensive career guidance.

  • What programs/sessions do we conduct?

    C.O.D.E is dedicated to supporting pre-final and final year students in preparing for interviews and placements, while HEADFIRST caters specifically to first-year students, offering guidance on understanding the fundamentals of data structures and algorithms crucial for competitive programming. Additionally, the club conducts periodic sessions that delve into emerging technologies. CODEX is a series of competitive programming contests to test the students’ understanding and coding skills. The Bell Labs are another series that provide in-depth sessions on the intricacies of operating systems and related concepts. Similarly, a interactive session known as Masterclass is organized with alumni, exploring insights into pursuing higher studies.

  • Are there any prerequisites for attending specific ACM-CEG programs or sessions?

    If any softwares or platforms are required, we will inform the participants priorly and provide them the necessary details of installation.

  • What is our aim?

    Our mission is to inspire students to embark on a transformative journey of learning and mastering the tools of computer science, cultivating a genuine passion for the discipline. We are dedicated to removing obstacles, offering clear guidance, and empowering students to achieve their goals in the field.

  • What is the full form of ACM?

    The full form of ACM is Association for Computing Machinery.

  • Is there a membership fee to join ACM-CEG?

    No, there is no membership fee to join ACM-CEG.

  • How to become a part of ACM-CEG?

    An open call would be conducted every year (during even semesters). Those who attend it can become a part of ACM-CEG. Wish to step towards the final era of the game, here is the final ticket which leads you to the finale. All the best !!!

  • How can one access the information posted, particularly regarding opportunities?

    Utilizing our Newsletter as a communication channel, we disseminate pertinent and valuable information within the network. Simply subscribe to our Newsletter section with your email, and receive timely updates on events and opportunities. Explore "Offcamp," our program dedicated to sharing off-campus opportunities, including scholarships, internships, job openings, and more, accessible through Instagram, and LinkedIn.

  • When was this club started in CEG?

    Established in 2004, the ACM-CEG Student Chapter is committed to fostering a steadfast passion for computer science among students. The club offers numerous networking opportunities and facilitates access to invaluable advice from leading experts in the field.
