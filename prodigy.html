WHO DUNNIT

Team Size : 1-2 members

Put your coding skills to the test in this thrilling event where teams must solve a mysterious murder through logic and code. Each coding challenge reveals a crucial clue, unlocking phrases that lead to a combination code for a mysterious box. Inside the box lies the final riddle—solve it, and you'll uncover the identity of the victim. The team that decodes, deduces, and cracks the case the fastest wins. Ready to be the top detective?


Cyber Dystopia

Team Size : 1-2 members

Cyber Dystopia features two thrilling rounds.
Round 1: Brain Battle, participants solve puzzles and logic challenges, unlocking hints to form a phrase for bonus points. Teams can also open mystery boxes for boosters or handicaps.
Round 2: CodeCombat, the top 8 teams face off in one-on-one coding battles, where mini-games determine the difficulty of the coding challenge. Winners get easier problems, while their opponents take on tougher ones. Mystery boxes add surprises to both rounds. Get ready for a dynamic mix of puzzles, strategy, and coding!


Ideathon

Team Size : 2-3 members

High school students, get ready to tackle real-world problems with creative product ideas! In teams of 2-3, you'll brainstorm and develop innovative solutions based on problem statements related to everyday challenges like environmental issues or health and wellness. Participants will outline their product idea, including the problem, solution, and a development roadmap. Top teams will refine their ideas and present detailed technical plans, followed by a Q&A with judges. Showcase your creativity, problem-solving skills, and compete to create the most impactful solution!


Family Feud

Team Size : 4-5 members

Join us for Family Feud, where teams of 4-5 students compete to answer fun, survey-based questions on topics like school subjects, pop culture, and general knowledge. The fastest team to buzz in gets the first chance to answer, earning points based on the popularity of their responses. If they answer incorrectly, the opposing team can steal. The team with the most cumulative points at the end wins!


Deconstructed Ladders

Team Size : 2-3 members

Get ready for a fun twist on Snakes and Ladders in Deconstructed Ladder! Teams race across a 40-space board by answering questions on general knowledge, science, and tech trivia. Buzz in first to answer, with correct answers moving your team forward 1-3 spaces, depending on the question's difficulty. The board is divided into three zones—Easy (1-10), Intermediate (11-30), and Difficult (31-40)—with increasing points and stakes. Watch out for bonus squares with high-risk challenges, and after Space 15, take a shot at Double or Nothing for a chance to soar ahead—or fall back!


Shipwrecked Tales

Team Size : 1 member

Put your creativity and quick thinking to the test in Shipwrecked Tales!
In Round 1: Convince the Captain, participants are assigned unique characters and have one minute to convince the captain to give them the last life jacket. br In Round 2: Storytelling Challenge, improvise a story about why the ship sank, with each member building on the narrative for one minute. Expect surprise twists like mystery words and genre switches. The most engaging and creative storytellers will win the day!
